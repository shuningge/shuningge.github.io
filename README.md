## About Me
Welcome! I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Political Science Department and IDSS (joint IDPS program) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, advised by Professors Naoki Egami, Daniel Hidalgo, Themistoklis Sapsis, Fotini Christia, and In Song Kim.

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and causal inference. Specifically, I hope to develop and apply computational methods and experimental frameworks for social scientists that use high-dimensional multimodal data like videos.

Before joining MIT, I worked as a Senior Data Coordinator at Penn Development Research Initiative, research assistant at MIT CSAIL, and was a co-founder of a start-up with $10 million Series A funding. I graduated with a B.Eng. in Material Science & Engineering and a B.B.A. in Management from Tsinghua University. I also hold an M.S. in Systems Engineering from the University of Pennsylvania.
